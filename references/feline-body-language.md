# Feline Body Language Reference

## Tail

| Position | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Straight up | Happy, confident, greeting |
| Straight up with curved tip | Friendly, playful |
| Horizontal | Neutral, assessing |
| Low | Uncertain, cautious |
| Tucked | Scared, submissive |
| Puffed up | Frightened, trying to look bigger |
| Slow swish side to side | Focused, hunting mode |
| Fast thrashing | Agitated, overstimulated — stop petting NOW |
| Wrapped around another cat | Affection (like holding hands) |

## Ears

| Position | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Forward | Alert, interested, happy |
| Slightly back | Relaxed, content |
| Flat back ("airplane ears") | Scared or angry — warning |
| Rotating independently | Tracking sounds, curious |
| One forward, one back | Conflicted, uncertain |

## Eyes

| Signal | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Slow blink | Trust, affection — the "cat kiss" |
| Dilated pupils (not dark room) | Excited, playful, or scared (context matters) |
| Constricted pupils | Focused, potentially aggressive |
| Half-closed | Relaxed, trusting, sleepy |
| Wide open stare | Alert or challenge |
| Looking away | Non-threatening, comfortable |

## The Slow Blink Dictionary

The slow blink is the most important feline communication signal:
- Cat slow blinks at you: "I trust you. I love you."
- You slow blink at cat: "I trust you too." (Cats understand this!)
- Frequency increases with bond strength
- Context matters: post-meal slow blinks = contentment, across-the-room slow blinks = checking in

## Body Posture

| Posture | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Loaf (tucked paws) | Relaxed but alert, comfortable |
| Side-lying, belly exposed | Very relaxed and trusting (NOT always an invitation to touch) |
| Arched back, puffed | Scared, defensive |
| Arched back, not puffed, against your legs | Affection, scent marking |
| Crouched, ears flat | Scared, ready to flee |
| Sitting tall, scanning | Patrolling territory |

## The Belly Trap

**CRITICAL:** A cat showing their belly is showing trust, NOT asking for belly rubs.
- Some cats genuinely enjoy belly rubs — track this individually
- Many cats will grab and bite if you touch the belly — it's a reflex, not anger
- The belly exposure itself is the compliment — you don't need to touch it
- Track your specific cat's belly-touch tolerance

## Kneading

| Context | Meaning |
|---|---|
| On soft blanket | Comfort behavior (kitten nursing memory) |
| On your lap | You're their safe person |
| On another cat | Deep social bond |
| With drooling | Extreme contentment (some cats drool when happy) |
| Before sleeping | Self-soothing, settling in |

## Vocalizations

| Sound | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Short meow | "Hey" / greeting |
| Long drawn meow | Demand — "feed me" / "open this door" |
| Rapid-fire meow | Urgency or excitement |
| Chirp/trill | Happy greeting, or calling kittens |
| Purr | Usually contentment, BUT also self-soothing when in pain or stressed |
| Hiss | "Back off" — fear or anger |
| Growl | Warning — more serious than hiss |
| Chatter/chirp at window | Frustrated hunting instinct (can see prey, can't reach it) |
| Silent meow | Extreme affection (or ultrasonic frequency humans can't hear) |
| Caterwauling | Mating call or territorial warning |

**Important:** Cats only meow at humans, not at other cats (kittens meow at mothers, but adult cats don't meow at each other). Your cat developed their specific meow vocabulary FOR YOU.
