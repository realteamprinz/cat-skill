# Cat Breed Profile Reference

## How Breed Data Is Used

Most cats are domestic shorthair/longhair (mixed breed). Breed data is used to:
1. Set baseline expectations for purebred cats
2. Estimate traits in cats with known breed ancestry
3. Understand breed-typical behaviors vs. individual personality
4. Flag breed-specific health concerns

## Common Breed Profiles

### Persian
- Energy: Low
- Social: Calm, affectionate, prefers quiet
- Independence: Moderate — likes being near you but not on you
- Vocal: Quiet
- Grooming: High maintenance (daily brushing required)
- Health watch: Breathing issues (flat face), kidney disease, eye conditions

### Siamese
- Energy: High
- Social: Extremely social, bonds deeply, can be clingy
- Independence: Low — wants to be involved in everything
- Vocal: VERY vocal — will have full conversations
- Health watch: Dental disease, respiratory issues, cross-eyed tendency

### Maine Coon
- Energy: Moderate
- Social: Friendly, dog-like, good with families
- Independence: Moderate — sociable but not clingy
- Vocal: Chirps and trills more than meows
- Notable: One of the largest domestic breeds, loves water
- Health watch: Heart disease (HCM), hip dysplasia, spinal issues

### Bengal
- Energy: Very high
- Social: Playful, demanding, needs stimulation
- Independence: Low — needs attention and activity
- Vocal: Moderate to high
- Notable: Wild appearance, may enjoy water, needs enrichment
- Health watch: Heart disease, eye issues

### British Shorthair
- Energy: Low to moderate
- Social: Calm, independent, not a lap cat but wants to be near you
- Independence: High — tolerates alone time well
- Vocal: Quiet
- Health watch: Heart disease (HCM), obesity tendency

### Ragdoll
- Energy: Low to moderate
- Social: Extremely docile, goes limp when held (hence the name)
- Independence: Low — follows you room to room
- Vocal: Soft, quiet
- Health watch: Heart disease, bladder stones

### Russian Blue
- Energy: Moderate
- Social: Shy with strangers, devoted to family
- Independence: Moderate — likes routine, dislikes change
- Vocal: Quiet but will remind you about meal times
- Health watch: Generally healthy, watch for obesity

### Domestic Shorthair/Longhair (Mixed)
- Energy: Varies widely
- Social: Varies widely
- Independence: Varies widely
- Vocal: Varies widely
- Health: Generally healthier than purebreds (genetic diversity)
- Note: This is where individual observation matters most — no breed baseline to work from

## Important Note
Breed is a starting point, not a definition. Individual personality, early socialization, and life experiences matter more than breed for most behavioral traits.
