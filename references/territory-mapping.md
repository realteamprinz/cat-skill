# Territory Mapping Reference

## What Territory Means to a Cat

Territory isn't just "where the cat goes." It's a complex map of:
- **Core territory:** Where they sleep, eat, and feel safest
- **Hunting territory:** Where they play/hunt (even indoor cats have this)
- **Patrol routes:** The paths they walk to check their territory
- **Perimeter:** The edges of their claimed space

## Mapping Dimensions

### Vertical Territory
Cats think in 3D. Map:
- Floor-level zones (under beds, behind furniture)
- Mid-level zones (chairs, couches, window sills)
- High zones (cat trees, top of bookshelves, refrigerator)
- **Height = status** in multi-cat homes

### Horizontal Territory
- Room-by-room preferences
- Time-of-day preferences (sun follows different paths)
- Seasonal changes (warm spots in winter, cool spots in summer)

### Window Stations
For each window the cat uses:
- What they watch (birds, squirrels, people, cars, other cats)
- Time of day they prefer each window
- Level of engagement (casual glance vs. chittering at prey)
- Whether they get agitated or relaxed watching

### Scratch Marking
Locations where the cat scratches are territory markers:
- Note each scratching location
- Track if scratching increases (may signal territorial stress)
- New scratching in unexpected spots = something changed in their territory sense

### Rubbing/Bunting Locations
When cats rub their face on objects, they're scent-marking:
- Track what objects get rubbed
- Note frequency changes
- New objects get rubbed to "claim" them
- People and other pets get rubbed too (claiming you)

## Territory Changes

Events that disrupt territory:
- New furniture (especially if it replaces a favorite spot)
- Rearranging rooms
- Guests staying over
- New pet introduction
- Construction noise
- New outdoor cats visible from windows
- Seasonal changes (heating/AC, sun angles)

## Multi-Cat Territory

In multi-cat homes, track:
- Shared zones vs. exclusive zones
- Time-sharing (one cat gets the spot mornings, another afternoons)
- Contested zones (where conflicts happen)
- Buffer zones (space between each cat's core territory)
- Resource placement relative to territory (food, litter, water should be in each cat's zone)
